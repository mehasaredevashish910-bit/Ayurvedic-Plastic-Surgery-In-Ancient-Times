Step One

Patient Preparation & Selection

रोगी परीक्षण एवं तैयारी

Before any surgical intervention, Sushruta prescribed a thorough examination of the patient — Rogi Pariksha. The surgeon assessed the patient's constitution (Prakriti), vitality (Bala), and mental fortitude. Only patients of strong constitution and stable mind were selected.

A pre-operative regimen of Snehana (oil massages) and Svedana (steam therapy) was administered for 3–7 days to soften tissues and improve circulation. The patient fasted the night before surgery.

🫒 Snehana (Oil Massage) ♨️ Svedana (Steam) 🌿 Dosha Balance 📿 Ritualistic Prayers 🌙 Night-before Fast

Step Two

Anaesthesia — The Ancient Sleep

संज्ञाहरण — मदकारी औषधि

Sushruta described the use of Sammohini — a potent herbal anaesthetic compound — to render patients unconscious before surgery. This is one of the world's earliest records of surgical anaesthesia.

🌿 Sammohinī Compound Cannabis (Vijaya) + Datura (Dhatura) + Wine (Madya) + Aconite (Vatsanabha) administered as a paste or inhalant to induce deep sleep and numbness.
🍃 Local Numbing Cold water packs, pressure bandaging, and herbal pastes of Sarshapa (mustard) and Pippali were applied to the operative site for local anaesthesia.
🌸 Cannabis (Vijaya) ☠️ Datura (Dhatura) 🍷 Fermented Wine ❄️ Cold Compression

Surgical Instruments — Shastra

शस्त्र — शल्यचिकित्सा उपकरण

Sushruta catalogued 101 blunt instruments (Yantras) and 20 sharp instruments (Shastras). Instruments were made of steel and tested for sharpness by shaving a hair. They were sterilised with boiling water and medicated smoke.

Step Four

The Incision — Chedana

छेदन — त्वचा का चीरा

Sushruta described eight types of incisions (Astha Chedana) suited to different anatomical regions. For rhinoplasty, the classic procedure involved measuring the exact size of the missing nasal portion using a parna (leaf) and transferring the pattern onto the forehead.

The incision was made swiftly and decisively — Sushruta wrote that a hesitant surgeon cuts poorly, while an overly bold one cuts dangerously. "Mridutvam Tikshnata ca" — gentleness and sharpness both.

📏 Leaf Template Measurement ✂️ Swift Single Stroke 🩸 Haemostasis Herbs 🌿 Turmeric Applied

Step Five

Flap Harvest — The Pedicle Technique

त्वक्-पट्टिका — नासिका पुनर्निर्माण

The jewel of Sushruta's genius — the forehead flap (median forehead flap) for nasal reconstruction. A flap of skin was raised from the forehead, keeping one end attached (pedicle) to maintain blood supply. This technique is still called the "Indian Method" of rhinoplasty in modern medicine.

The pedicle preserved the supratrochlear vessels. Sushruta understood that keeping the blood supply intact was essential for flap survival — a concept re-discovered in the West only in the 19th century.

🧬 Forehead Flap Raised 🩸 Pedicle Preserved 🌱 Blood Supply Maintained ⚗️ Herb Paste Applied 🔁 "Indian Method" (Still Used)

Step Six

Reconstruction & Suturing — Sivana

सीवन — घाव का मिलाप

The harvested flap was folded and sutured onto the nasal defect. Sushruta described suture materials made from natural fibres — Shana (hemp), Munja grass, horsehair, and strips of intestine. Suturing was done with precision using the curved Suci needle.

Small tubular reeds (nala) were inserted into the nostrils to maintain patency and allow breathing during healing. The raw surfaces were sprinkled with powdered liquorice, red sandalwood, and barberry to prevent infection.

🪡 Suture Materials Hemp thread, Munja grass fibre, horsehair, silk, and bark strips — chosen based on wound depth and body location.
🌿 Reed Stents Inserted 🪡 Hemp Sutures 🌸 Sandalwood Powder 💛 Liquorice Dusting

Step Seven

Wound Dressing — Vrana Bandhana

व्रण बन्धन — पट्टी एवं लेपन

Post-operatively, the wound was covered with medicated oils infused with Tila (sesame) and Ghrita (clarified butter). Bandaging was done in 14 recognised patterns based on body part — the forehead and nose required the Shiroband (head bandage) and Nasya-band.

The wound was inspected daily. Signs of healthy healing (Shuddha Vrana) included absence of foul smell, minimal discharge, and pink granulation tissue. Infected wounds were treated with medicated fumigation and anti-microbial herbal pastes.

🧈 Ghrita (Clarified Butter) 🌿 Sesame Oil Dressing 🩹 14 Bandage Types 🔥 Medicated Fumigation 🌡️ Daily Inspection

Step Eight

Recovery & Post-operative Care

स्वास्थ्य लाभ — पुनःस्थापना

Recovery followed a strict Pathya regimen — a therapeutic diet and lifestyle code. The patient avoided spicy, sour, and heavy foods. Light meals of rice porridge with ghee, milk, and easily digestible vegetables were prescribed.

The pedicle connecting the flap to the forehead was severed only after 2–3 weeks once the flap had fully vascularised. Over weeks, the nose took its final shape. The donor site on the forehead healed with minimal scarring due to meticulous herbal application.

🌸 Post-op Herbal Protocol Triphala washes for wound cleansing · Turmeric + Neem paste for antimicrobial protection · Brahmi for nerve healing · Ashwagandha for strength recovery.
🍚 Pathya Diet ✂️ Pedicle Severed (Week 2–3) 🌿 Triphala Wash 💛 Turmeric Healing 🧘 Complete Rest
